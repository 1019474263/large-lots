The City of Chicago is selling vacant lots on the South Side for $1. Here's how you get one.

The Large Lot Program will open again for East Garfield Park starting July 1st, 2014. Check back here for more information.

Step 1) Find a City-owned, residential lot on your block

Enter your address or browse on the map below to find the lot(s) you would like to purchase.

The city is only selling lots that are residential and have no buildings on them.
Each lot has its own 14-digit PIN number. Write it down for your application.

To get started:

  1. Enter your address to view available lots near you
  2. Click on a lot on the map for details

Step 2) Make sure you're eligible

  • You must own property on the same block as the lot you want. To prove this, you need to include a copy of your property deed in your application.
  • The lot must be owned by the City. It has to be vacant - no buildings allowed. And it needs to be zoned residential - that means you can't put a store or office there, only housing.
  • You can apply for up to two lots on your block.
  • You can't owe the City any money. You need to have paid all property taxes, water bills, parking tickets, and any other obligations to the City.
  • After you buy the lot, you need to own it for at least five years before selling it.
  • You'll need to maintain the lot in accordance with all local building, housing and zoning codes and ordinances. The Department of Planning and Development strongly encourages potential applicants to review the ordinances pertaining to Weeds and Lot Maintenance and Fencing
  • You need to include an Economic Disclosure Statement (EDS) along with the application. This is a legal document the City uses to learn more about you or your organization before signing a contract with you.
  • If you are a non-profit, include your articles of incorporation and certificate of good standing from the State of Illinois with the application.
  • Churches are not eligible.

Step 3) Put together your application

To purchase a lot, you need to put together an application package to mail to the City of Chicago's Department of Planning and Development.

This package will include:

  1. Large Lot Application Form
  2. Copy of your Deed
  3. Economic Disclosure Statement
  4. If a non-profit organization, articles of incorporation and certificate of good standing from the State of Illinois

1. Large Lot Application Form

Download and fill out the Large Lot Pilot Program application form.

2. Copy of your Deed

The City requires that you submit a copy of your deed to prove that you already own property on the block. If you don't have it handy, you can get it online or pick it up in person.


Download a copy from the Cook County Recorder of Deeds

You can download a copy from the Cook County Recorder of Deeds website for $2.50.

You will need to know the PIN of your own property. You can look it up by your address on CookCountyPropertyInfo.com.


Or pick it up in person

You can also go to the Cook County Recorder of Deeds office and get a printed copy.

Cook County Recorder of Deeds
118 N. Clark St, Room 120
Chicago, IL 60602

3. Economic Disclosure Statement

Everyone who does business with the City of Chicago needs to fill out an Economic Disclosure Statement (EDS), and since you are buying a lot from them, you need to fill one out too.

Many of the sections on the form are for businesses, so fill it out to the best of your ability according to the instructions.

Step 4) Submit your application

The Large Lots Pilot Program ended on April 21st, 2014 and the City of Chicago is no longer accepting applications.

The Large Lot Program will open again for East Garfield Park starting July 1st, 2014. Check back here for more information.

Frequently asked questions

How much in property taxes will I pay?

The amount of yearly property tax you will pay is determined by the Cook County Assessor and based on the assessed value of the property and the tax rates for all local governments providing services in your community.

To get a sense of what the property tax will be for your vacant lot, we took a look at the taxes paid for a few vacant lots sold by the City of Chicago:

Address Sq ft Assessed value 2012 taxes Sold on
5302 S Winchester Ave 2,892 $15,900 $287.65 Sept 8, 2011
745 N Cicero Ave 3,305 $28,268 $548.01 Jul 6, 2011
1319 S Tripp Ave 3,142 $31,420 $563.81 Apr 13, 2011
1326 W 109th St 3,730 $20,510 $368.83 March 9, 2011
Will I have to pay back taxes?

No you will not. When the lot is sold, no unpaid taxes by the previous owner will be transferred to you.

About

LargeLots.org was initiated by Demond Drummer at Teamwork Englewood, built by DataMade and funded by LISC Chicago with support from the Boeing Corporation, and the Knight Foundation for LISC's OpenGov for the Rest of Us project.

Our map was built using open data from Chicago and Cook County:

  • Chicago - City Owned Land Inventory
  • Chicago - Wards
  • Cook County - 2012 Parcels

We prepared some extracts of the above sources for this project. You can download those here:

  • Complete listing of lots displayed in the map (CSV)
  • Geospatial definitions of the lots displayed above (Shapefile)
  • Boundary of the Large Lots Program area (Shapefile)

All the code for this site is open source and available on Github. We used the following open source tools:

  • QGIS - graphic information system (GIS) desktop application
  • PostGIS - geospatial database
  • Bootstrap - Responsive HTML, CSS and Javascript framework
  • Leaflet - javascript library interactive maps
  • CartoDB - platform for rendering interactive map tiles
  • jQuery Address - javascript library creating RESTful URLs
